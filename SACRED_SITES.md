# Luo Kai: Open World — Sacred Sites

## Purpose
Sacred sites are where the world’s spiritual logic becomes visible. They are not just pretty locations; they are places that affect behavior, atmosphere, and requests.

## Sacred site categories
### Shrine
- local, practical, often maintained by villagers
- used for offerings, requests, blessings, and grief
- common in Spirit Forest and smaller settlements

### Temple
- more formal, more organized, more tied to doctrine
- may be watched or controlled by sect influence
- becomes more common in higher-tier regions

### Ancestral hall
- family or village memory space
- tied to names, lineage, and duty
- useful for lore and world history requests

### Meditation stone or platform
- simple but spiritually dense
- used by cultivators to concentrate qi
- often placed where the land feels quiet and open

### Peak altar
- high-realm, exposed, and ceremonial
- used for breakthroughs, rituals, or tribulations
- should feel serious and rare

## Sacred site design rules
- each sacred site should have a purpose that people in the world understand
- sacred sites should not be interchangeable
- some should be small and human
- some should be large and institutionally controlled
- sacred sites should react to time, weather, and the player’s cultivation state

## Sacred site atmosphere
### Ordinary shrine
- incense smoke
- worn wood
- bells, candles, paper talismans
- quiet foot traffic

### Active shrine
- brighter glow
- more people
- stronger wind or subtle qi motion
- offerings visible

### Neglected shrine
- cracked stone
- faded paint
- dead incense burners
- more spiritual instability nearby

### Elevated site
- longer sightlines
- stronger bloom or aura
- ritual objects arranged with intent
- more silence around the site

## Sacred site world memory
- restoring a site should visibly change it
- completed requests may add new offerings, lights, or ward stones
- a site damaged by spirit activity should stay visibly scarred until healed

## Request links
Sacred sites are ideal request anchors for:
- offerings
- purification
- relic recovery
- prayer or vow quests
- cultivation teachings
- spirit pacification

## Why sacred sites matter
They make the world feel like it has a spiritual infrastructure, not just combat spots. The player should understand that qi is part of a lived culture, not merely a resource bar.
