# Luo Kai: Open World — Zone 2: Mist Ridge

## Purpose
Mist Ridge is the first zone that should make the player feel the world is getting larger and older. The geography should become more vertical, more exposed, and more spiritually tense.

## Identity
- Foundation Establishment influence begins here
- cliffs, wind, fog, switchbacks, and hanging paths
- spirits are more organized and more dangerous
- the zone feels like a threshold between mortal living and sect territory

## What the player should feel
- distance
- fragility
- weather as a navigational force
- the first real sense of moving into a larger world

## Visual language
- slate stone
- pale mist
- narrow trails and rope bridges
- weathered stone markers
- cliffside pines
- distant temple silhouettes
- brighter spiritual lines in rocks and shrines

## Landmarks
1. Wind Stair
2. Mist Gate
3. Broken Watchtower
4. Cliff Shrine
5. Echo Cave
6. Ridge Lantern Line

## Spatial layout
- lower trail from Spirit Forest
- central ridge road with travel hazards
- side ledges and hidden shelves
- cave pockets tucked into cliff walls
- one hard climb that later becomes a shortcut
- one camp shelf that serves as a believable rest point

## Ambient life
- heavy wind noises
- rope and wood creaks
- distant falling stones
- bird circles over cliffs
- fog swallowing and revealing shapes
- night lights from shrine and campfires

## NPC presence
- training NPC near the cliff shrine
- hunter NPC on the road
- scavenger or messenger at the watchtower
- occasional sect observer who does not fully trust the player

## Request themes
- deliver supplies to the shrine
- clear dangerous ridge beasts
- recover a lost talisman from the cave
- prove body strength on the staircase
- escort a messenger through the fog

## Enemy behavior
- more ambushes
- vertical attack angles
- enemies that push the player off balance
- stronger reaction to noise or light

## World memory
- a repaired rope bridge stays visibly repaired
- a cleared ledge remains safer
- a completed shrine request changes lanterns or ward stones
- a defeated cave guardian leaves a visible scar

## World story
Mist Ridge should feel like a place where ordinary villagers stop going unless they have a reason. It is the first zone that makes the player feel the world is bigger, older, and less forgiving.
