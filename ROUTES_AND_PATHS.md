# Luo Kai: Open World — Routes and Paths

## Purpose
Paths should tell the player what kind of journey they are taking.

## Route types
### Safe road
- broadest and most maintained
- used by families, merchants, and patrols
- slower to build, but safest

### Trade route
- used by carts, pack animals, and merchants
- connects production to markets
- often has road posts and resting points

### Patrol route
- optimized for visibility and control
- may avoid narrow cuts if they reduce sight lines
- used by guards, sect observers, or escorts

### Hunter route
- follows tracks, water, and animal movement
- often partly hidden
- usually rougher and more dangerous

### Spiritual route
- path to shrines, ritual sites, or cultivation grounds
- may be marked with stone, lanterns, or talisman strips
- can be safer or more dangerous depending on local qi pressure

## Route readability rules
- the player should be able to tell the main road from side paths without a UI arrow
- routes should bend around landmarks, water, and hills in believable ways
- if a route is secret, there should be environmental clues
- if a route is dangerous, the danger should be visible before the player enters it

## Route progression
As the player grows stronger or helps the world:
- hidden paths become known
- broken paths become repaired
- short cuts become available
- old road posts reopen
- new paths open into later zones

## Route memory
The world should remember route changes.
- a bridge repaired once should stay repaired
- a cleared track should remain easier to read
- a secret path should be present after discovery
- a blocked route should stay blocked unless the story changes it

## Travel pacing
Travel should alternate between:
- clear sightlines
- turning points
- safe pockets
- danger pockets
- landmark arrivals

That rhythm makes the world feel like geography instead of a corridor.
