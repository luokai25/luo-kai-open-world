# Luo Kai: Open World — Villages and Camps

## Purpose
Each village and camp needs a real reason to exist and a believable relation to the land.

## Village principles
- villages form around water, trade, shrine access, and defensible ground
- a village should show what it produces and what it fears
- homes should cluster near safety and convenience
- the edge of the village should feel like the beginning of the wild, not a hard line

## Village parts
### Hearth core
- cooking, sleeping, family life
- mostly private spaces
- simple fences, stacked wood, drying racks

### Work ring
- storage, tools, laundry, repair, deliveries
- where most daily errands happen
- usually near roads or water

### Public edge
- gate, shrine, board, watch post, well, or ferry point
- where travelers and requests appear
- a social threshold between insiders and outsiders

## Camp principles
- camps are temporary or semi-permanent
- camps form where work or danger requires staying overnight
- camps should reflect the job of their occupants

## Camp types
### Hunter camp
- meat, hides, traps, scent markers
- near forest edge or beast routes
- includes drying racks, a fire ring, and track-reading posts

### Road guard camp
- watch duty, signal fire, spare tools, report board
- near passes, bridges, or road junctions
- more orderly than a hunter camp

### Cultivator camp
- meditation mats, talisman paper, qi stones, quiet space
- usually in secluded places with strong natural qi
- may be temporary while the cultivator waits for a breakthrough

### Work camp
- ore, timber, river stones, bridge repair, road clearing
- exists because a project is happening
- should disappear or transform when the work is complete

## Social logic
- villagers know the local forest and weather patterns
- camps are for people who choose risk or necessity
- the player should always be able to tell whether a place is permanent or temporary

## World memory in villages and camps
- a camp emptied after a completed request should look abandoned or repurposed
- a village saved from danger should gain visible signs of confidence: repaired fence, new lanterns, better stockpiles
- a camp that suffered attack should show damage until the player helps

## Real-feeling daily behavior
- villagers sweep, fetch water, cook, and talk in the morning
- camps prepare gear at dawn
- people eat and resupply at midday
- work slows in evening
- both villages and camps get quiet at night

## How villages and camps differ
- villages are about continuity
- camps are about function
- villages are places the player can return to repeatedly
- camps are places defined by task, danger, or movement
