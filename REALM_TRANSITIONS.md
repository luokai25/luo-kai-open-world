# Luo Kai: Open World — Realm Transitions

## Goal
When the player gets stronger, the world should feel different, not just the numbers.

## Transition rules
A breakthrough should change at least one of the following:
- visual mood
- NPC dialogue
- enemy confidence
- landmark glow
- available request types
- traversal comfort
- area ambience

## Mortal -> Qi Gathering
World changes:
- mist becomes more noticeable
- spirit motes appear near old roots and shrines
- NPCs start noticing the player’s aura
- weak spirits become easier to defeat

World feeling:
- the player has stepped into the spiritual side of the world
- ordinary life is still dominant, but not fully

## Qi Gathering -> Foundation Establishment
World changes:
- stonework and shrine symbols feel more important
- guards and elders become more formal
- more vertical paths and mountain routes become relevant
- travel begins to feel like a skill rather than a walk

World feeling:
- the player is no longer a novice
- the world starts to recognize discipline and permanence

## Foundation Establishment -> Core Formation
World changes:
- lights become richer and more concentrated
- sect areas become more accessible
- NPCs respond with more respect
- special materials and rare routes become visible

World feeling:
- the player is becoming part of the cultivation hierarchy

## Core Formation -> Nascent Soul
World changes:
- stronger atmospheric bloom
- quieter spaces feel sacred or uncanny
- some world spaces become dreamlike
- spiritual disturbances become more common

World feeling:
- the world is no longer just physical geography
- the player is crossing into deeper metaphysics

## Nascent Soul -> Soul Formation
World changes:
- ruins, sigils, and memory-like visuals become more prominent
- NPCs speak in more cautious or reverent ways
- strong enemies may retreat or test the player differently
- rare requests become more personal or symbolic

World feeling:
- the player is seen as a serious force

## Higher realms
At higher realms, the world should feel ceremonial, unstable, and important.
- stronger weather
- stranger landmarks
- more ritual spaces
- fewer but weightier NPC interactions
- more direct environmental storytelling

## Realm feedback examples
### Visual feedback
- brighter shrine light
- stronger horizon haze
- altered particle density
- more prominent aura effects around the player

### Social feedback
- villagers lower their voices
- elders speak more directly
- merchants become more willing to share information
- guards stop blocking paths that they used to challenge

### Spatial feedback
- shortcuts become easier to understand
- hidden routes reveal themselves more clearly
- previously intimidating routes become manageable

## Why this matters
Cultivation should feel like a relationship with the world, not a menu of stat upgrades. The player should break through and then immediately feel the world answer back.
