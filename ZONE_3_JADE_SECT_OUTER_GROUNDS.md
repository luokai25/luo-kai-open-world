# Luo Kai: Open World — Zone 3: Jade Sect Outer Grounds

## Purpose
This zone should make the player feel that the world has institutions, rules, and formal access. It is where cultivation becomes organized, not just personal.

## Identity
- Core Formation influence
- guarded architecture
- discipline, rank, and ceremony
- more polished stonework and strict spatial order

## What the player should feel
- status matters
- the world is governed by power and procedure
- access is earned
- the sect is not a dungeon; it is a society

## Visual language
- clean stone terraces
- jade accents
- lantern courtyards
- wall banners and ward pillars
- training fields and storage halls
- formal gates and controlled approaches

## Landmarks
1. Sect Outer Gate
2. Lantern Courtyard
3. Disciples’ Training Yard
4. Archive Steps
5. Artisan Forge Row
6. Status Bridge

## Spatial layout
- outer public approach roads
- guarded entry gates
- open courtyards for meetings and training
- side buildings for artisans, storage, and lodging
- restricted inner paths that require reputation or realm

## Ambient life
- disciplined foot traffic
- hammering from forge spaces
- chanting or drills at training yard
- bells signaling routine changes
- incense and paper talismans near gates

## NPC presence
- sect gatekeeper
- trainer or instructor
- artisan or blacksmith
- disciples in small groups
- merchants with permits
- an elder or hall attendant

## Request themes
- deliver materials to the forge
- prove body control in training
- recover missing sect records
- escort a permitted merchant
- clean a nearby spirit disturbance under supervision

## World memory
- access badges or reputation visibly matter
- completed tasks open side routes or hall access
- the player is recognized differently after repeated help
- areas change from locked to permitted

## World story
Jade Sect Outer Grounds should make the player understand that cultivation is not only power. It is also institution, rank, access, and obligation.
