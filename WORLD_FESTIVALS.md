# Luo Kai: Open World — World Festivals and Rituals

## Purpose
Festivals make the world feel historical and communal rather than purely functional.

## Festival roles
Festivals should do more than decorate the calendar.
- reveal local belief
- move NPCs into shared spaces
- create temporary requests
- change lighting and sound
- show how people interpret cultivation and danger

## Seasonal events
### Spring Awakening
- new herbs appear
- shrine offerings begin
- villagers clean roads and gates
- early qi activity feels gentle

### Summer Watch
- travel is busiest
- river crossings matter
- hunters are more active
- storms become a bigger concern

### Autumn Offering
- harvest, trade, and shrine gratitude
- more incense and memorial activity
- roads feel full of deliveries

### Winter Vigil
- fewer travelers
- more fires and shelter spaces
- spiritual disturbances feel closer
- stronger emphasis on endurance and protection

## Zone rituals
### Spirit Forest
- shrine incense renewal
- bridge blessing
- forest appeasement offering

### Mist Ridge
- wind prayer
- lantern line lighting
- cliff ward repair

### Jade Sect Outer Grounds
- discipline assessment
- martial demonstration
- formal lineage ceremony

### Soul Vale
- remembrance rite
- dream lantern release
- silence observance

### Ascension Peak
- summit vow
- tribulation preparation
- final ritual circle

## NPC participation
Rituals should move NPCs into shared spaces.
- elders speak publicly
- merchants pause travel
- hunters bring trophies or warnings
- artisans present repaired tools
- children and villagers appear in safe spaces

## Player role in rituals
The player should not just watch.
- deliver offerings
- clear a path
- protect the rite
- bring qi stones or rare items
- prove strength or resolve
- trigger a world-state change

## Why it matters
Rituals show that the world is lived in collectively. They make the player feel like they entered a culture, not just a landscape.
