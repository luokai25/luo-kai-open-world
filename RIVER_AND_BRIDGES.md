# Luo Kai: Open World — River and Bridges

## Purpose
Rivers shape the world more than they decorate it. They create boundaries, routes, trade lines, ritual sites, and danger zones. Every river in the world should have a specific job.

## The Three-River System

### Spirit River (Shallow, Eastern)
- runs through Spirit Forest
- shallow enough to ford in three places
- one bridge: Broken Spirit Bridge (partially collapsed)
- used for washing, gathering, and ritual offerings
- herb gatherers work near its banks
- calm during the day, slightly luminescent at night during high qi
- the ford at the village edge is a social point (traders, children, resting travelers)

### Mist River (Faster, Central)
- cuts through the base of Mist Ridge
- deeper, harder to cross
- one rope bridge (the Mist Crossing) maintained by the road warden
- one narrow stone ford only passable in dry weather
- feeds Echo Cave through an underground channel
- the sound of the river acts as an audio landmark in fog
- spirits gather near its deeper pools

### Jade Channel (Controlled, Western)
- runs through Jade Sect Outer Grounds
- engineered sections: stone channels, sluices, small dams
- sect disciples maintain its flow
- used to power small water wheels for forge work
- one ceremonial white stone bridge guarded by sect disciples
- the channel marks the official boundary into higher sect territory

## Bridge design language
- **Wooden footbridge**: village tier, narrow, creaks, rope rails
- **Stone arch (broken)**: once stronger, now precarious, story-rich
- **Rope bridge**: mountain tier, wind-swayed, visibility-limited, exposure-high
- **Ceremonial stone bridge**: sect tier, wide, clean, guarded, status-gated
- **Natural ford**: low risk when clear, impassable in storms or snow

## River as boundary
- Spirit River separates safe village edge from deeper forest
- Mist River separates mortal travel from sect-adjacent territory
- Jade Channel separates public sect grounds from restricted inner sections

## River as resource
- herb growth near shallow banks
- qi condensation near deep pools at night
- spirit stones exposed in riverbed after floods
- forge water drawn from Jade Channel for crafting

## River as danger
- Mist River rising during storms blocks the crossing and isolates travelers
- deep pools in all three rivers become spirit nests at night
- flooded paths after heavy rain change the available routes and force detours

## River and world memory
- a repaired bridge should show visible repair work (newer wood, fresh rope, fresh stone)
- a dried ford after a dry season should look cracked, dusty, and exposed
- a flooded crossing should leave behind debris, mud lines, and washed-away campsite remnants
- a river cleaned of spirits should look calmer and clearer afterward
