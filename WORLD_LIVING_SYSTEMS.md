# Luo Kai: Open World — World Living Systems

## Purpose
Define the living systems that make the world feel active even when the player is not fighting.

## Systems overview
The world should simulate small things that matter:
- time of day
- weather
- NPC schedules
- local danger
- request availability
- resource presence
- world memory

## Time of day
Time should change the world visibly.
- **Dawn**: smoke rises, incense is lit, people leave home
- **Morning**: markets open, roads fill, public requests appear
- **Afternoon**: labor peaks, travel is common, resource gathering intensifies
- **Evening**: people return, lanterns appear, social spaces fill up
- **Night**: roads thin, danger rises, secret activity becomes possible

## Weather
Weather should affect mood and behavior.
- **Clear**: travel is easy, landmarks are visible, settlement life feels open
- **Fog**: routes feel uncertain, spirits feel nearer, hidden paths become important
- **Rain**: roads slow down, river crossings matter, sound becomes softer
- **Storm**: rare, spiritual, and dangerous; people seek shelter
- **Wind**: exposes height, makes ridges feel alive, emphasizes distance

## NPC schedules
NPCs should have simple but believable routines.
- elders stay near shrines and thresholds
- merchants move between trade points
- hunters patrol edges, camps, and forest borders
- trainers work in yards and practice spaces
- artisans stay near production spaces
- wandering cultivators drift between camps and ruins

## Zone state
Each zone should track:
- spiritual pressure
- local threat
- settlement activity
- known landmarks
- request conditions

These values do not need to be complex. They just need to make the world react consistently.

## World memory
Important events must leave traces.
- a bridge repaired should look repaired
- a shrine restored should look active
- a den cleared should leave signs of the struggle
- a request completed should change dialogue and place behavior
- a broken route should remain blocked until a story reason changes it

## Resource ecology
Resources should feel like they belong.
- herbs near moisture and old trees
- qi stones near spiritually active ground
- ore near cliffs and exposed rock
- charcoal and fuel near industry or camps
- relic fragments in ruins and forgotten sites

## Danger ecology
Danger should emerge from place.
- forest spirits near old roots and shrines
- beasts on trails and at water edges
- ridge creatures near cliffs and wind tunnels
- vale spirits in unstable, memory-like spaces
- peak trials at ritual heights

## Settlement activity
Settlements should not stay static.
- shops open and close
- guards rotate posts
- NPCs eat, sleep, and move around
- roads are busier by day
- safe zones feel safer for a reason

## World messages
The game can surface world living information in subtle ways:
- current time
- weather
- zone pressure
- landmark text
- local rumors
- active request hints

## Why it matters
A living world is not one big simulation. It is a set of small, consistent signals that make the player believe the place is real.
