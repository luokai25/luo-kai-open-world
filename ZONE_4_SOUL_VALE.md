# Luo Kai: Open World — Zone 4: Soul Vale

## Purpose
Soul Vale should feel like the world is remembering things incorrectly. It is the first zone where reality becomes spiritually unstable rather than just geographically difficult.

## Identity
- Nascent Soul influence
- dreamlike valleys and quiet ruins
- memory, echoes, and spiritual imbalance
- fewer people, more meaning in each presence

## What the player should feel
- unease
- mystery
- echoes of history
- the world itself is becoming less ordinary

## Visual language
- violet haze
- silver glints
- long shadows
- broken ceremonial walls
- reflective pools
- stone platforms half buried in mist

## Landmarks
1. Moon Ruin
2. Dream Lake
3. Echo Tower
4. Silent Steps
5. Spirit Mirror Pool
6. Memory Arch

## Spatial layout
- folded valleys with hidden pockets
- routes that circle or double back
- ruins that reveal themselves in layers
- reflective water that helps orient the player
- one deep hidden place only reachable after advancement

## Ambient life
- distant chime-like resonance
- wind that sounds almost like voices
- water with unnatural stillness
- shifting fog lines
- occasional spiritual pulse at night

## NPC presence
- rare lore keeper
- wandering cultivator
- ritual caretaker
- maybe one broken survivor or exile

## Request themes
- restore a memory relic
- carry a rite object to the mirror pool
- investigate a voice in the ruin
- clear a wandering spirit
- collect a rare fragment from a dream site

## World memory
- restored memorials stay restored
- disturbed places stay visibly disturbed until repaired
- completed rites change the soundscape and light
- the valley responds to cultivation strength

## World story
Soul Vale should feel like a place where the land itself keeps emotional residue. The player is no longer just traveling through terrain; they are moving through history and memory.
